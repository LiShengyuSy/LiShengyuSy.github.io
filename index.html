About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), working with Professor Mo Li.

I received my Ph.D. in 2025 from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC), under the supervision of Professor Li Lu.

Earlier, I obtained my B.E. degree in 2019 from the Xi’an University of Posts and Telecommunications (XUPT).

My research interests lie in system security, wireless and physical‑layer security, and acoustic security. I have actively participated in several key and general projects funded by the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC), achieving notable outcomes in domains such as physical‑layer threat detection, wireless bus architectures, and IoT security. As the first or primary author, I have published seven papers at leading venues including ACM MobiCom and IEEE TDSC, and am currently applying for three national invention patents. Through extensive research experience in system design and security, I emphasize a balance between theory and practice, aspiring to address real‑world system security challenges with innovative solutions.

My ongoing research focuses on intelligent localization and sensing using wireless signals. By leveraging ambient signals—including various radio frequencies (e.g., Wi‑Fi, millimeter wave) and acoustic waves—I develop methods to perceive and interpret environmental dynamics. I explore the evolution of IoT technologies, particularly Wi‑Fi, Bluetooth, and LoRaWAN, to meet practical industrial needs, emphasizing robust network reliability and optimized Quality of Service (QoS).
