About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), working with Prof. Mo Li. I received my Ph.D. in 2025 from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC), under the supervision of Prof. Li Lu. Earlier, I obtained my B.E. degree in 2019 from the Xi’an University of Posts and Telecommunications (XUPT).

My research focuses on the security and reliability of modern wireless and acoustic systems. I am interested in understanding how signals can be both exploited and protected in the physical layer, and in building systems that remain robust when faced with real‑world channel dynamics and adversarial interference. More specifically, my work explores system security, wireless and physical‑layer security, and acoustic security. I study how information is transmitted over the air—from RF to ultrasonic bands— and how vulnerabilities can emerge in these processes. By combining signal processing, wireless communication, and system design, I develop methods for secure wireless communication, cross‑layer sensing, and signal‑based threat detection.
